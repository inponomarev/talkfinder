JPoint 2018 (06.04.2018 — 07.04.2018)

Big data in modern biology

Mikhail Gelfand

Using the examples of recent years researches, we’ll discuss the problems and prospects of the new science being developed — system biology, as well as how modern methods of data analysis can be used in it. The talk will be elementary from all points of view and does not require any prior knowledge.

With the development of high-performance experimental methods, molecular biology has entered the path, which astrophysics and high-energy physics follow for a long time so far: it has become a data-rich science.

Instead of studying single genes and proteins, one can now try to study the work of a cell in general.

However, everything has its own price: on the one hand, there is a lot of noise in the data. Оn the other hand, instead of concise biological facts, artificial constructs and models become the object of research; the level of abstraction often exceeds the one the biologists are comfortable with.

Using the examples of recent years researches, we’ll try to discuss the problems and prospects of the new science being developed — system biology, as well as how modern methods of data analysis can be used in it. That said, the talk will be elementary from all points of view and does not require any prior knowledge.
